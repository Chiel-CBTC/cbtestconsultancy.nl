# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences, roughly equal weight:

1. **IT managers and CTOs** at mid-to-large organisations (finance, retail, energy, government) — evaluating whether Chiel has the seniority and track record to embed in their team. They skim quickly; they want proof, not promises.
2. **Recruiters and intermediaries** — checking availability, rate, and fit for a client assignment. They need to extract facts fast and move on.

Both arrive with limited time. The site must establish credibility within seconds and make contacting easy.

## Product Purpose

CB Test Consultancy is Chiel Bleumink's solo QA consulting practice — 17+ years of experience in test automation, Playwright, and AI-powered QA, serving clients including Rabobank, TenneT, Action, Provincie Gelderland, Europarcs, and Gadero.

The site exists to convert qualified visitors into a first conversation. It is not a passive brochure — it should read as the work of someone who takes precision seriously.

Success: a visitor lands, immediately reads "senior, reliable, precise", and reaches out.

## Brand Personality

Senior · Precies · Betrouwbaar (Senior · Precise · Reliable)

Voice: direct, no buzzwords, no over-promising. Chiel is confident because the track record speaks — not because the copy shouts. Tone is professional but human; not corporate-anonymous.

## Anti-references

- Cheap freelancer energy (Fiverr/Upwork aesthetic — crowded, commodity-feeling)
- Generic WordPress/template look — forgettable, indistinguishable
- Over-designed agency sites — too many colors, too much motion, style over substance
- Corporate anonymity — big-firm blandness with no personality

## Design Principles

1. **Precision signals expertise.** The care visible in the UI is a direct signal of the care Chiel brings to QA work. No rough edges, no lazy defaults.
2. **Show seniority; don't claim it.** Client names, real outcomes, and concrete skills carry more weight than adjectives. Let the evidence do the talking.
3. **Confident restraint.** White space is not emptiness — it is authority. Don't fill for the sake of filling. Less, done well.
4. **Direct over decorative.** Every element must earn its place. If removing it makes the page clearer, remove it.
5. **Consistent craft at every scale.** A misaligned margin or a vague CTA label undermines the "senior" read as much as bad architecture does.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Basic good readability as the floor: sufficient contrast, clear hierarchy, no motion that blocks content. No formal WCAG level required, but the client base includes public-sector organisations — AA contrast ratios are the sensible default.
